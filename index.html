Understanding COPPA: Critical Impact on Kids Content Monetization

What is COPPA?

The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act is a U.S. federal law enacted in 1998 (updated 2013) that protects children under 13 by requiring verifiable parental consent before collecting personal data. Following the 2019 FTC settlement with YouTube ($170M fine), all "Made for Kids" content became subject to strict compliance requirements effective January 2020.

Why It Matters for Karazah

Karazah Channel is classified as "Made for Kids" content, triggering automatic COPPA compliance. This fundamentally changes monetization potential compared to general audience channels, requiring adjusted revenue projections and valuation methodology.

COPPA Impact on YouTube Monetization
Feature General Content Made for Kids Revenue Impact

Valuation Implication: All revenue projections in this document use COPPA-adjusted CPM/RPM rates ($0.20-$0.55 vs. general audience $3-$5). Subscriber valuations are similarly adjusted ($0.50-$0.75/sub vs. $2-$4/sub for general audience channels). This is why diversification beyond YouTube ad revenue (apps, live shows, licensing) is critical for value creation. Sources: COPPAcalypse Study (SSRN), FTC Settlement, Creator RPM Data
